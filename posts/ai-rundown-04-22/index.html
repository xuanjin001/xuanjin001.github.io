Credo Technology Unveils 224G PAM4 SerDes IP on TSMC N3 — Doubling AI Connectivity Speeds to 1.6 Tb/s Link to heading

source

Credo Technology has launched its 224G PAM4 SerDes IP on TSMC’s N3 process — doubling the data rate of its previous 112G generation to 1.6 terabits per second. The new IP is designed to eliminate bandwidth bottlenecks that slow down training and inference in large AI models, critical for the hyperscale data centers that power today’s AI workloads. Gartner estimates the Scale-Up AI Fabrics market will reach $13 billion by 2028, and Credo’s announcement positions it squarely in the middle of that scaling race. This matters because faster interconnects mean AI clusters can train larger models more efficiently without hitting networking walls.

Google Cloud Commits $750 Million to Accelerate Partners’ Agentic AI Development Link to heading

source

Google Cloud announced a $750 million fund to help its 120,000-member partner ecosystem build and deploy agentic AI solutions for joint customers. The fund supports prototyping, agent building, deployment, upskilling, and embedded Google forward-deployed engineers. Key partners including Accenture, Bain, BCG, Deloitte, and McKinsey will get early access to Gemini models — their feedback will help refine the models for enterprise use cases. Nine AI-native services partners — including Altimetrik, Artefact, and Deepsense — will launch dedicated Gemini Enterprise practices under the program. Google is building a full agentic AI channel, and this fund is designed to make partners the delivery vehicle.

Anthropic’s New Mythos AI Model Sets Off Global Alarms — Central Banks and Intelligence Agencies Respond Link to heading

source

Anthropic’s Mythos AI model has triggered emergency responses from central banks and intelligence agencies globally, as the company carefully controls access due to serious safety and security concerns. Mythos has been shared with over 40 organizations that operate critical global infrastructure — including Amazon, Apple, and Microsoft — to help identify and fix security vulnerabilities the model identifies. Anthropic has been deliberate about keeping the model small and restricted, suggesting the risks are not theoretical. The episode reflects a new phase in AI governance: major labs now visibly weighing national security implications before wide release.

SpaceX Secures Right to Acquire Cursor for $60 Billion — Or $10 Billion for Ongoing Joint Work Link to heading

source

SpaceX has struck a deal giving it the right to acquire AI coding startup Cursor later this year for up to $60 billion — or alternatively pay $10 billion for the work the two companies are doing together under the “SpaceXAI” partnership. SpaceX and Cursor are already working closely to build “the world’s best coding and knowledge work AI,” according to a joint post on X. Cursor, which has quickly emerged as a serious player in AI-assisted coding, is also raising $2 billion in parallel funding from Andreessen Horowitz, Nvidia, and Thrive Capital. The deal signals that SpaceX is serious about integrating AI across its operations beyond rockets and satellites.

Classover and ICreate Partner on Hands-On AI and Robotics Learning Environments in North America Link to heading

source

Classover, an AI-powered education company listed on NASDAQ, has announced a strategic collaboration with ICreate Education Technology Co., Ltd., a leading AI education robotics company serving over one million users globally. ICreate was recognized with top honors at the 2026 Bett Awards for its AI Coding Companion. The partnership aims to co-develop hands-on AI and robotics learning environments for students across North America, integrating ICreate’s robotics hardware and AI-driven curriculum with Classover’s AI-powered learning platform. The goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical AI knowledge and real-world applied learning — giving students actual robots to program, not just concepts to read about.
