Senator Joshua Hawley’s GUARD Act, a bill designed to protect children from the potential dangers of AI chatbots, has unanimously passed a congressional committee vote. This legislative push highlights a growing anxiety around kids and technology, especially as platforms like YouTube increasingly replace traditional school curricula. While schools are trying to mitigate digital distractions by banning cell phones, experts note this is only a partial fix since kids remain constantly connected outside of class. Ultimately, the push underscores a mounting urgency among lawmakers and educators to establish stronger boundaries between minors and pervasive digital technologies. source
