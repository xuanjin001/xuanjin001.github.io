Another tech company says it will cut hundreds of jobs amid pivot to AI Link to heading

Groupon just announced it will cut up to 400 jobs—nearly a quarter of its workforce—as part of a plan to become an “AI-native” platform. This is the latest in a massive wave of tech layoffs that has seen over 800,000 workers lose their jobs since 2022, with companies like Meta and Coinbase also citing AI as a driving force behind the cuts. The shift matters because it signals that even established tech firms are aggressively restructuring to prioritize automation over human labor, reshaping the entire industry’s job market. For context, laid-off workers have even formed hiking groups to network and support each other through career transitions, highlighting just how widespread and personal this trend has become. source

A.I. Doesn’t Have to Mean Layoffs Link to heading

Schneider Electric is bucking the trend by using AI to boost worker productivity instead of cutting jobs, while many CEOs are celebrating mass layoffs as a sign of AI success. The company’s approach aligns with economists like Erik Brynjolfsson, who argue that replacing workers misses the bigger opportunity for growth. This matters because it challenges the dominant narrative that AI’s main value is slashing labor costs, offering a blueprint for how companies can innovate without sacrificing their workforce. source
